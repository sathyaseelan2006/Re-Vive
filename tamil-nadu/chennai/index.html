Site Overview

Chennai, formerly known as Madras, stands as the cultural capital of Tamil Nadu and one of India's most historically significant cities. From ancient temples showcasing magnificent Dravidian architecture to the colonial grandeur of Fort St. George, Chennai represents a perfect fusion of traditional Tamil heritage and modern metropolitan culture. The city's strategic location on the Coromandel Coast has made it a center of trade, culture, and learning for over 2,000 years.

Historical Legends

The Sacred Land

Mylapore, one of Chennai's oldest districts, is steeped in ancient legends and religious significance, with stories of divine manifestations that have made it a sacred place of worship for centuries.

St. Thomas Connection

Mylapore is believed to be where St. Thomas the Apostle lived and was martyred in 72 CE. The blend of Christian and Hindu heritage makes this area unique in India's religious landscape.

Urban Legends

Mylapore's Ancient Legacy

According to ancient legends, Mylapore derives its name from the Sanskrit word 'Mayilapur', the city of peacocks. The area has been associated with divine stories and sacred traditions for over two millennia, giving it the name - the city of peacocks.

The Apostle's Sacred Ground

St. Thomas the Apostle is believed to have lived and preached in Mylapore, making it one of the earliest Christian settlements in India. Local folklore speaks of miraculous healings and divine interventions that occurred during his time, blending Christian and Hindu traditions in this sacred land.

The Vanishing Fort Treasure

Colonial legends speak of a hidden treasure buried somewhere within Fort St. George by the last Mughal governor before the British takeover. Despite numerous searches, this treasure has never been found, leading many to believe it was protected by ancient Tamil guardian spirits.
